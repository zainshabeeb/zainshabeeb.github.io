About

I’m a PhD candidate in Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering at Georgia Tech working on AI for Nanoscience. I build physics-informed generative and discriminative models for electron microscopy data, with a focus on LPTEM and cryo-EM—segmentation, super-resolution, and simulation of nanoparticle and protein dynamics.

My recent work includes a Nature Communications (2025) paper on physics-informed generative modeling for LPTEM and accepted contributions to the NeurIPS AI4Science workshop. I like clean, reproducible pipelines that bridge rigorous physics with modern deep learning (VAE/Transformer, diffusion models) to make nanoscale measurements more informative and data-efficient.

I’m broadly interested in problems at the interface of materials, biotech, and imaging: learning from low-SNR measurements, compressed acquisition, and building models that respect physical constraints.

Selected Projects

LEONARDO — Physics-informed Transformer-VAE for LPTEM

Georgia Tech · 2024–2025

End-to-end pipeline to learn and simulate nanoparticle diffusion from LPTEM, with metrics on kurtosis/variance matching, IPSD alignment, and domain-specific evaluation.

CryoGEN — Compressed sensing with diffusion priors for cryo-EM

Georgia Tech · 2024–2025

Diffusion-prior guided reconstruction for high-frequency recovery from compressed/low-dose cryo-EM measurements; focuses on data-efficiency and frequency-domain fidelity.

MoNet 2.0 — Trajectory-based classifier for nanoscale motion

Georgia Tech · 2024–2025

Feature-learning and probabilistic metrics for labeling anomalous diffusion regimes in LPTEM trajectories; supports evaluation of generative trajectories (FID-style on features).
