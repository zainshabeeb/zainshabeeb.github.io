About

I’m a PhD candidate in Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering at Georgia Tech working on AI for Nanoscience in the Jamali Lab. I build physics-informed generative and discriminative models for electron microscopy data, with a focus on LPTEM and cryo-EM, including segmentation, super-resolution, and simulation of nanoparticle and protein dynamics.

My recent work includes a Nature Communications (2025) paper on physics-informed generative modeling for LPTEM and accepted contributions to the NeurIPS AI4Science workshop. I like clean, reproducible pipelines that bridge rigorous physics with modern deep learning (VAE/Transformer, diffusion models) to make nanoscale measurements more informative and data-efficient.

I’m broadly interested in problems at the interface of materials, biotech, and imaging: learning from low-SNR measurements, compressed acquisition, and building models that respect physical constraints.
